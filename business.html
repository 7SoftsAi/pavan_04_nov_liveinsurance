1.1 INSURANCE FOR SOLE PROPRIETOR

1.1.1 MWP TRUST

In the event of the proprietor's death, illness, or disability, an insurance policy under the MWP Act can:

  • Protect the family from sudden loss of income
  • Prevent family disputes over business succession
  • Guard against unexpected claims from unaccounted creditors
  • Help retain skilled technical employees
  • Prevent forced liquidation and business closure
  • Shield the family from financial decline

1.1.2 EMPLOYEE RETENTION SCHEME

Difficulties faced by a Businessman:

  • Retention of employees for a long period of time
  • What benefits can be offered to the employee
  • Managing new recruitment and training costs
  • Compensation for the death of an employee

Employer - Employee Insurance:

Employer-Employee Insurance, also known as the Employee Retention Scheme, will be beneficial for retaining employees. The employer can gift any LIC Policy to the employee, paying the premiums, and the benefits of the policy are given to the employee.

Benefits to Employer:
  • The premium paid can be claimed as a business expense
  • Taxable income reduces
  • Employee Welfare
  • Employee stays loyal to the company, increasing employee retention
  • A big fund is accumulated in the name of the employee, gaining their trust
Benefits to Employees:
  • Helps the employee secure the future for themselves and their family
  • Provides risk cover, including:
    • Natural Death Cover
    • Accidental Death Cover
    • Disability Cover
  • Potential for a huge fund or pension provisions through the policy

1.2 INSURANCE FOR PARTNERSHIP FIRM

1.2.1 Life Insurance Funded, Valuation-Based Buy-Sell Agreement

Disadvantages of Partnership Firms:

  • Unlimited risks, disagreements, dissolution
  • Disputes among partners may lead to liquidation
  • In the event of a partner's death/disability:
    • The deceased family may demand income without putting in efforts
    • A new partner from the deceased family may not be compatible with other partners
    • Remaining partners may not have the funds to buy the deceased partner's share
    • Dissolution of the partnership in case of no agreement

Solution:

Insurance Funded Buy-Sell Agreement is a proper solution. The firm takes life insurance policies on the lives of the partners, with the firm as the beneficiary. In case of a partner's death, the insurance amount is used to buy out the partner's share from the heirs.

Benefits to Partnership Firm:
  • Prevents disputes among partners
  • Facilitates smooth succession planning
  • Provides liquidity to the firm
  • Ensures financial stability
Benefits to Partners:
  • Provides financial security to the partner’s family
  • Enables the continuation of business operations
  • Prevents financial burden on the remaining partners

1.5 INSURANCE PLANS FOR SCHOOLS

1.5.1 Raasta Aapatti Kavach Policy

Highlights:

  • The policy offers PERSONAL ACCIDENT compensation cover, including reimbursement of Hospitalization expenses incurred due to an accident.
Scope of Cover:
  • Section I - The policy offers Personal Accident compensation cover with a Sum Insured ranging from INR 25,000 to INR 1 lac and further multiples of INR 1 lac up to INR 10 lacs.
  • Section II - Hospitalization Expenses for bodily injury caused by and arising out of an accident, including:
    • Road Accident (at additional premium)
    • Arising out of and during the course of employment (if opted for at additional premium)
    • Any other accident (wider cover) (if opted for at an additional premium)
Sum Insured:

The Sum Insured ranges from INR 25,000 to INR 1 lac and further multiples of INR 1 lac up to INR 10 lacs for Personal Accident as well as Hospitalization expenses. The hospitalization limit allowed shall be equal to and up to the maximum Accident limit.

Premium:

Premium depends upon the Sum Insured chosen. The policy is also available on a group basis. The above is only a broad indication of the cover offered. For further details, contact any of our Policy issuing offices.

1.6 INSURANCE PLANS FOR SHOPKEEPERS

1.6.1 Highlights:

This is a package policy specially designed for small shopkeepers. It is a single policy combining the various insurance requirements of shopkeepers. A discount in premium is available if a minimum number of four sections is selected, including Section I (b).

Only one policy can be taken by one shopkeeper for each shop in a specific location having separate books of accounts.

Scope:

The policy comprises the following 11 sections:

  • Section I - Building & Contents: Covers shop building and/or contents against loss or damage caused by Fire & Allied perils, such as Fire, lightning, explosion of gas in domestic appliances, etc.
  • Section II - Burglary & Housebreaking: Covers contents of insured shop premises (excluding money and valuables) against loss or damage by burglary and/or housebreaking.
  • Section III - Money Insurance: Covers loss of money in transit, loss of money/valuables whilst contained in a locked safe, loss of money contained in cashier's till and/or counter by burglary/housebreaking.
  • Section IV - Pedal Cycles: Covers loss/damage to pedal cycles belonging to insured by Fire, lightning, or external explosion, riot, strike, malicious or terrorist act, etc.
  • Section V - Plate Glass: Covers loss of or damage to fixed plate glass in insured's shop by accidental means.
  • Section VI - Neon Sign/Glow Sign: Covers loss of or damage to neon sign/ glow sign by accidental external means, Fire, lightning, external explosion, theft, riot, etc.
  • Section VII - Baggage: Covers baggage carried by the insured in connection with shop trade against loss/damage by accident or misfortune during transit anywhere in India.
  • Section VIII - Personal Accident: Covers accidental bodily injury resulting in death, permanent total disablement, or loss of limbs/eyes.
  • Section IX - Fidelity Guarantee: Covers financial loss caused by fraud or dishonesty of the employee of the insured.
  • Section X - Liability: Covers legal liability to third parties for accidental bodily injury and/or accidental damage to property.
  • Section XI - Public Liability: Covers liability to third parties for accidental bodily injury or damage to property arising out of insured's business.
